I am a Senior Quality Engineering professional with 7+ years of experience across EdTech, Electric Vehicles, and Aviation Leasing — and I am now making a deliberate, structured transition into Product Ownership. At LeaseWorks (a Portside / Vista Equity Partners company), I led quality engineering on Aeris, a Salesforce-based aviation leasing SaaS platform, where my work extended well beyond testing: I owned backlog health, co-authored acceptance criteria, led UAT sign-off across finance, contracts, and asset management stakeholders, and directly influenced release sequencing through defect impact data.

With a Master's degree in Software Systems from PSG College of Arts and Science, I have built deep expertise in Salesforce testing, AI-augmented QA workflows — including a bespoke framework for Salesforce Agentforce using Cursor AI and MCP protocol — and Agile/Scrum delivery. I have consistently operated at the intersection of business requirements and engineering reality: the same intersection where great Product Owners live.

My transition into Product is grounded in practice, not just intent. I completed the AirTribe PM Launchpad programme (Capstone: TrulyMadly TM Café, May 2026), developed end-to-end PRDs, RICE-scored backlogs, OKR frameworks, and GTM strategies across six product exercises spanning consumer tech, HRTech, and SaaS. I am currently pursuing my Certified Scrum Product Owner (CSPO) certification.

What I bring to a Product Owner role is something most candidates cannot offer: I have been the person who catches what gets missed when requirements are unclear, scope is unchecked, and acceptance criteria are vague. I know exactly how product decisions ripple into engineering effort and release quality — and I am ready to make those decisions from the other side of the table.
